## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I have to follow all the rewording suggestions exactly?**  
A: No. The suggestions are starting points. You know your thesis best—customize them to match your voice while preserving the source accuracy. Just ensure the reworded claim can be defended against the actual PDF text.

**Q: Some options suggest replacing a source. Is that okay?**  
A: Yes, if the new source (e.g., Tsai instead of Knijnenburg) more directly supports the claim. This is how you fix audit mismatches.

**Q: What if I disagree with a weak_support rating?**  
A: The audit uses automated text matching (RapidFuzz), so false negatives are possible. If you believe a claim is actually well-supported, you can keep it as-is and let the re-audit show improvement. The Priority 4 (Papadakis) is an example—the claim is likely correct but the audit couldn't find textual proof.

**Q: Can I reword claims to be vaguer/weaker to pass the audit?**  
A: You could, but that would weaken your thesis, not strengthen it. The goal is to make claims match source evidence, not to dodge audit scrutiny.

**Q: Do I have to fix all 17 claims?**  
A: Priority: Do Priorities 1–6 first (HIGH impact). Then Priorities 7–17 (MEDIUM impact) if time allows. Priorities 1–6 are central to your narrative.

**Q: What if fixing a claim makes it say something I don't agree with?**  
A: Then the source doesn't support your current claim. Either find a better source or reframe the claim to match what the source actually says. This is part of good scholarship.
