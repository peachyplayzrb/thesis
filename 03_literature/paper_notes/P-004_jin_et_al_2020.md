research_problem:
How user characteristics influence the effectiveness of control-oriented interfaces in music recommender systems.

method_or_system_type:
Music recommender UI research with experiments on controls and visualizations.

key_findings:
- One-size-fits-all control interfaces are suboptimal.
- Musical sophistication influences acceptance/diversity outcomes for control and visualization designs.
- Control design must consider cognitive load and individual differences.

limitations:
- Focuses on interface/control behavior, not full deterministic pipeline engineering.
- Findings may depend on studied user groups and interface variants.

relevance_to_thesis:
Direct relevance for controllability layer design and parameter exposure strategy.

gap_implications:
Supports controllability objective but warns that control mechanisms must be deliberately designed and evaluated.

design_implications:
- Avoid exposing excessive controls by default.
- Add staged or profile-based control presets to reduce cognitive overload.
