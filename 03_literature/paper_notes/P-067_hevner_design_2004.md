research_problem:
Establish a rigorous design-science paradigm for information systems research so artifact-building work is evaluated with explicit, defensible research guidelines rather than ad hoc implementation claims.

method_or_system_type:
Foundational design science research paradigm and evaluation-guidelines paper

key_findings:
- Distinguishes behavioral-science and design-science paradigms and argues both are foundational for IS research quality.
- Frames design-science knowledge production as building and evaluating purposeful artifacts under explicit relevance and rigor expectations.
- Provides guideline-driven criteria for evaluating design-science contributions and their evidence quality.
- Emphasizes that utility and justification claims must be bounded to demonstrated artifact context and evaluation surface.

limitations:
- Methodology-level paper; does not provide music-recommender-specific implementation or controllability mechanisms.
- High-level guidance requires project-specific operationalization through concrete artifact contracts.
