research_problem:
Define a rigorous, communicable methodology for design science research in information systems so artefact construction and evaluation are linked through explicit research activities.

method_or_system_type:
Foundational design science research methodology paper

key_findings:
- Formalizes a six-activity DSRM flow spanning problem identification, objective definition, design and development, demonstration, evaluation, and communication.
- Argues that design science contributions should be justified through explicit linkage between problem relevance, artefact design, and evaluation evidence.
- Positions methodology as a way to make artefact-building research systematic rather than ad hoc.
- Supports presenting design decisions as traceable responses to prior problem analysis rather than as isolated implementation choices.

limitations:
- Methodology-level source rather than music-recommender evidence.
- Does not resolve recommender-specific trade-offs around transparency, controllability, or cross-source uncertainty by itself.

relevance_to_thesis:
Anchors the Chapter 3 methodological position by justifying the move from Chapter 2 tensions to explicit design requirements, architecture choices, and later evaluation evidence.
