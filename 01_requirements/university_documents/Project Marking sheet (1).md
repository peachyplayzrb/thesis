## Dissertation and artefact criteria

- **Literature Search and Review**: range, quality, relevance, comparison, evaluation, synthesis, demonstrated understanding, references and bibliography.
- **Report Presentation**: clarity of ideas, grammar and spelling, fluent writing style, structure aligned to the project handbook.
- **Artefact Analysis**: requirement elicitation and relevance to aims and objectives.
- **Design**: diagrams such as UML or ERD where appropriate.
- **Implementation**: completeness, relevance, quality, and challenge of the artefact. Demonstration or viva must pass.
- **Testing**: suitable methodology and tools, plus strong analysis of results linking back to research and aims.
- **Conclusions and Critical Evaluation**: answer the academic question based on research and testing, address aims and objectives, and evaluate the artefact and process with balanced argument.

## Overall mark

Calculated weighted average of the two marks above:

`Project Management mark * 0.2 + Dissertation and Artefact mark * 0.8`
