# Project - Professionalism and Project Module

1. This handbook only covers the Project part of the module.
2. The project subject matter must be related and appropriate to the course being studied. The project should address an academic issue and answer an academic question.
3. All students must follow the development life cycle, with the aim of producing an artefact such as software, hardware, or an algorithm developed to meet a specification.
4. To pass the module, a student must meet the BCS requirements.
5. The project must be practical and problem-solving in nature.
6. The project must include an artefact.
7. The project must include a development cycle, including testing.
8. The project must justify the development tools and techniques used.
9. The project must be related to the student's course of study.
10. There is no BCS resit; however, it is still possible to pass the module.
11. Students must include relevant supporting work to demonstrate the quality and accuracy of the work produced.
12. Each student is allocated a supervisor who acts as mentor and first marker.
13. Each project is also allocated a reader who provides support and marks the final project submission.
14. Students are required to produce a report containing a literature review, artefact details, the answer to the academic question, supporting work, conclusions, and critical evaluation.
15. Project management skills must be demonstrated throughout the project via regular meetings, logbook entries, a Gantt chart, and milestone completion.

## British Computer Society - Chartered Institute for IT Accreditation

1. The course is BCS accredited, meaning students can apply to join the BCS without sitting entrance examinations, subject to eligibility.
2. BCS accreditation confirms that the degree meets the academic requirements for later registration as a Chartered or Incorporated Engineer.
3. The BCS expects a substantial problem-solving project providing a major contribution to computing-related practical abilities.
4. To be eligible to apply for accreditation, the project must be passed at the first attempt.

# Ethical Considerations

Students should complete the Ethical Consideration section of the Project Registration form.

- If the project has no or limited ethical implications, no further action is required.
- If the project has potential ethical issues, the student must complete the Faculty Ethics Student Form and submit it via Canvas.
- If ethical approval is delayed, students should continue with non-risky project work while any risky elements remain on hold.

# Professionalism and Project Submissions and Assessments

Dates of submission are shown on the Canvas topic homepage.

1. To pass the module, students must pass both Assessment 1 and Assessment 2.
2. Professionalism Assessment 1 is worth 25% and is summative.
3. Project registration must be submitted via Canvas once a supervisor has agreed to supervise the project.
4. If a student cannot secure a project or supervisor in time, a Project Clearing Form must be submitted instead.
5. Ethical Considerations Form must be submitted if applicable.
6. Milestone 1 is the Project Proposal Report (Pass / Pass with minor modifications / Fail).
7. Milestone 2 is the Literature Review section (formative).
8. Milestone 3 is Artefact Design and Test Plan (formative).
9. Milestone 4 is the Professionalism section and early prototype (formative).
10. Milestone 5 is the draft report and advanced artefact version (formative).
11. Milestone 6 is the final report and final artefact, worth 75%.

The final report should typically be around 10,000 words, with 8,000 to 12,000 words considered acceptable. The artefact demonstration is compulsory.

# Supervisors and Readers

Supervisors are expected to mentor students, monitor progress, check logbook entries, and provide marking and feedback. Readers provide support when needed, attend the artefact demonstration, and mark the final report.

# Resit and Retake Requirements

Students scoring below 40% on the final project submission will receive feedback and may resubmit during the resit period. Students required to retake the project must complete the entire process again with a new project.
