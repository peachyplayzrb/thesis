# Finding Academic Information

Library and Information Services

<!-- Slide number: 2 -->
# Aims

- Use online catalogue (LibrarySearch)
- Use subject databases
- Use the web
- Evaluate resources

<!-- Slide number: 3 -->
# Starting your search

- Start with your reading list.
- Search the library catalogue when you want broad coverage.
- Search subject databases when you need subject-specific material.

<!-- Slide number: 4 -->
# Why do we need to find information?

- Provide evidence
- Demonstrate reading
- Generate ideas
- Pass your assignment

<!-- Slide number: 5 -->
# Why do you need a search strategy?

- Stay in control of your search.
- Be clear about what information you need.

<!-- Slide number: 6 -->
# Search strategy - think about

- Understanding the question
- Time available
- Amount of information required
- Search words and keywords
- Type of resources

<!-- Slide number: 7 -->
# What information?

The lecture prompts students to decide what kinds of sources and evidence are actually needed before searching.

<!-- Slide number: 8 -->
# Library catalogue (LibrarySearch)

The library catalogue searches broadly across resources.

<!-- Slide number: 9 -->
# Subject databases

Subject databases are selected by the academic support team, are often full text, and are usually accessed via Canvas or subject resource pages.

<!-- Slide number: 10 -->
# Generating search words

Consider:

- Subject words
- Alternative words
- UK/US spellings
- Focus terms such as country or date

<!-- Slide number: 11 -->
# Search terms

When identifying search terms, think of:

- Synonyms
- Alternative terms
- Alternative spellings
- Alternative formats

<!-- Slide number: 12 -->
# Search options

Different databases offer different limiters, commonly:

- Full text
- Peer reviewed
- Date
- Language

<!-- Slide number: 13 -->
# Using keywords

Think about the level of description and possible alternative terms.

<!-- Slide number: 14 -->
# Boolean logic

- `AND` narrows results.
- `OR` broadens results.
- `NOT` excludes results.

Examples given include `Car AND Motorbike`, `Car OR Motorbike`, and `Car NOT Motorbike`.

<!-- Slide number: 15 -->
# Truncation

Use `*` to search for words with multiple endings.

Example: `Business*` could find `businessman`, `businesswomen`, and similar forms.

<!-- Slide number: 16 -->
# Phrase searching

Examples:

- `"social media"`
- `"driverless cars"`

<!-- Slide number: 17 -->
# Wildcard searching

Example: `Organi?ation` can find both `organisation` and `organization`.

<!-- Slide number: 18 -->
# Wildcard searching

Example: `Behavio#r` can find both `behaviour` and `behavior`.

<!-- Slide number: 19 -->
# Search options

Extra limiters may include:

- Gender
- Ethnicity
- Age
- Geographical area
- Methodology
- Document type

# Reading journal articles

Tips:

- Read the abstract first.
- Look for keywords.
- Check reference lists for related reading.

<!-- Slide number: 23 -->
# Library catalogue or databases?

The lecture compares the two approaches.

<!-- Slide number: 24 -->
# Library catalogue - LibrarySearch

Advantages:

- Searches all subject areas and publications at once.
- Good broad starting point.

Disadvantages:

- Advanced search can be limited.
- Not subject specific.

<!-- Slide number: 25 -->
# Subject databases

Advantages:

- More relevant, subject-specific results.
- Advanced search often available.

Disadvantages:

- You may need to search multiple databases.

Students are directed to try a worksheet task using databases.

<!-- Slide number: 27 -->
# Searching the web

Use the web carefully and evaluate what you find.

<!-- Slide number: 28 -->
# Be careful using web resources

Risks include:

- Over-reliance when library resources may suffice.
- Anyone can publish online.
- Content can change or disappear.
- There may be no formal publishing process.
- Poor sources may cost marks.

<!-- Slide number: 29 -->
# When to use the web

Suitable uses include:

- Quick reference
- Organisations and societies
- News and directories
- Supplementing academic research
- Google Scholar
