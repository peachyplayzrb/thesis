# Contribution Statement

Chapter 2 establishes a persistent evidence gap in music recommender research: optimization-centric studies and accountability-centric studies are often evaluated separately, which leaves weak joint evidence about how preference uncertainty, candidate exclusion logic, explanation fidelity, and reproducibility constraints interact in one pipeline. This thesis addresses that gap by engineering a deterministic playlist-generation artefact where these interactions are explicit and testable rather than hidden in aggregate output quality.

The contribution is an auditable multi-stage pipeline design and evaluation frame that makes three aspects inspectable together: uncertainty in cross-source preference inference, controllable trade-offs in candidate generation and playlist assembly, and mechanism-linked explanation/reproducibility evidence at run level. The claim is intentionally bounded to engineering evidence under single-user deterministic scope, and does not claim model-family superiority or benchmark-state-of-the-art performance.
