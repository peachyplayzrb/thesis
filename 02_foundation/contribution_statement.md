# Contribution Statement

Current recommender research commonly evaluates accuracy, explanation, controllability, and reproducibility as isolated dimensions, leaving no clearly evidenced end-to-end playlist pipeline in the reviewed literature that engineers these qualities jointly under real cross-source data constraints. This thesis addresses that gap by implementing a deterministic, feature-based playlist generation artefact with separately auditable stages, each with defined inputs, outputs, and logged behavior, covering cross-source history alignment and profile construction, candidate filtering, feature-based scoring, playlist assembly, explanation generation, and run-level observability.

This artefact demonstrates that transparent, controllable, reproducible, and observable playlist generation can be co-engineered through explicit stage boundaries, configuration-driven controls, and documented alignment handling without requiring machine-learning model novelty. The contribution is intentionally bounded to a single-user, deterministic, content-based pipeline and is evaluated as a design demonstration: primarily through controllability scenarios and reproducibility checks, and supported by explanation/traceability outputs, observability logs, and playlist-rule compliance results, rather than as a performance claim against learning-based systems.
